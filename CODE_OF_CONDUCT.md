# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

This repository exists to help people navigate IT careers with honest, practical guidance. We are committed to providing a welcoming and supportive environment for everyone, regardless of background, experience level, education, or career path.

## Our Standards

**Positive behavior includes:**
- Being respectful and professional in all interactions
- Providing constructive feedback and suggestions
- Acknowledging different career paths and educational backgrounds
- Sharing knowledge and resources generously
- Assuming good intent from contributors

**Unacceptable behavior includes:**
- Gatekeeping or elitism ("you can't be a real developer without a CS degree")
- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment
- Promotional spam for bootcamps, courses, or companies
- Discrimination based on background, education, age, or career path
- Dismissing self-taught or career-changing contributors

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All repository interactions (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Any public or private communication related to this project

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue or contacting the repository maintainer. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from common open source community standards, tailored for an educational career resource.
